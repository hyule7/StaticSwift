StaticSwift is a studio of one. Harry Yule, working from Manchester, hand-coding websites for UK trades from a quiet desk. No agency overhead. No account managers. No subscription you can't leave.

Why the studio exists

Plumbers shouldn’t be paying agency rates. Or Wix rents.

The UK web is built for two kinds of buyer. The £30/month Wix tenant — locked in forever, paying for a template they never own. And the £3,000 agency project — sold the dream, delivered the spreadsheet, billed for change requests.

Neither serves a plumber in Letchworth or a barber in Bromsgrove well. Both leave you in a worse position than when you started: paying somebody else for a thing you can’t move.

StaticSwift exists in the gap. £499 to build it. £49 a month for hosting, care, and the occasional edit. Cancel any time. Files are yours forever.

And one promise above the rest: if it doesn’t produce a customer enquiry inside sixty days of going live, full refund — and you keep the site. Because I think the work earns it. If it doesn’t, you shouldn’t have to argue with me to get the money back. So I made it automatic.
