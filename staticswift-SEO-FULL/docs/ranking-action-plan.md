# Ranking action plan — SEO, GEO, AEO

Honest framing first: the on-site work is strong and now done (schema, llms.txt,
GBP entity wired, AI crawlers allowed, 31k long-tail pages). Average position 37
and one ChatGPT click is NOT a code problem. It is an AUTHORITY and AGE problem.
A young domain with near-zero organic and zero reviews cannot be willed to #1.
Google and AI engines rank and cite sources that are linked to, clicked, and
trusted. Everything below builds that. None of it is guaranteed; all of it is
the genuine path. No black-hat: a penalty would set you back further than
slow ranking.

## Where you are being let down (the real gaps)

1. **No reviews.** For a local service business this is the single biggest
   suppressor. GBP listings with reviews outrank empty ones almost every time,
   and AI engines cite businesses with social proof. You have your first orders
   now: get those clients to review.
2. **Near-zero backlinks.** Nothing points at the domain, so Google has no
   reason to trust it over established competitors. Authority is borrowed from
   who links to you.
3. **Thin index coverage risk.** 31k programmatic pages on a new domain: Google
   may index only a fraction and treat the rest as low-value. Pages that are not
   internally linked are inventory, not assets.
4. **No real proof on the site.** The trust mechanism is the free preview, but
   first real case studies (with permission) will lift conversion AND rankings.

## Do these, in order

### 1. Reviews engine (highest ROI, this week)
- Ask every paying client for a Google review the day you deliver. A direct
  link removes friction: get it from your GBP > Ask for reviews > copy link.
- Target 5 reviews fast, then a steady drip. Respond to every one.
- Put the same link in the delivery email and the client portal.

### 2. Google Business Profile optimisation
- Primary category: "Website designer". Add secondary: "Internet marketing
  service", "Marketing agency".
- Fill every field: services (list each: plumber websites, electrician
  websites, etc.), service areas (your real radius), hours, description,
  10+ photos of real work/screens.
- Post weekly (GBP posts): a build, a tip, an offer. Keeps the listing active.
- Make sure NAP (name "StaticSwift", phone +44 7502 731 799) matches the site
  EXACTLY. It now does in the schema.

### 3. Citations (10–20 that matter for UK)
List the business consistently (same NAP) on: Yell, Thomson Local, FreeIndex,
Cylex, Scoot, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect, Trustpilot, Clutch,
DesignRush, Bark, Checkatrade-style trade directories where eligible. Consistent
citations are a core local ranking factor.

### 4. Backlinks / digital PR (the slow compounding lever)
- Every client site you build: a tasteful "Website by StaticSwift" credit
  linking back (with their permission). This is the cleanest scalable link
  source you have, and it compounds with every sale.
- Guest angle: write one genuinely useful piece for a UK trades publication or
  blog ("why tradespeople lose jobs to a bad website").
- Get listed in "best web designers in Manchester" roundups.

### 5. AEO / GEO content (to turn 1 ChatGPT click into many)
- AI engines cite clear, factual, well-structured answers. Add a few deep,
  honest comparison/answer pages: "How much should a tradesperson's website
  cost in the UK?", "Wix vs hand-coded for trades", "Do I need a website if I
  have Facebook?". Answer the question in the first paragraph, with specifics.
- These rank for question queries AND get lifted into AI Overviews / ChatGPT.
- Keep llms.txt current (it is good now).

### 6. Index coverage (technical, measured)
- In Google Search Console: check Pages > Indexed vs Not indexed. If most of
  the 31k are "Discovered, not indexed", the estate is too thin/unlinked for
  Google to bother. Fix by strengthening internal links from hubs to leaves and
  pruning the weakest pages. Do this conservatively (SEO Director sign-off).

## Realistic timeline
- Weeks 1–4: reviews + GBP + citations. Local pack movement is the fastest win.
- Months 2–4: backlinks from delivered client sites compound; question/answer
  content starts getting cited and ranking.
- Ongoing: every sale = a backlink + a review + a case study. The flywheel.

The fastest, cheapest growth is not chasing #1 on head terms; it is turning each
client into a review, a link, and a referral. That is what moves 37 toward the
top and gets you cited by AI.
